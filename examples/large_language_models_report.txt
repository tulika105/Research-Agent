## **Overview**
Large Language Models (LLMs) are deep-learning models that process and generate natural language, with applications in various domains. They are built on transformer architectures, trained on massive corpora, and capable of a wide range of language-centric tasks. LLMs have become increasingly important in recent years, with significant advancements in their development and deployment.

## **Key Findings**
* LLMs are foundation models that can be adapted to many downstream tasks, including zero-shot, few-shot, and fine-tuned tasks.
* The transformer architecture, introduced in 2017, has replaced RNNs, enabling parallel training and longer context windows.
* LLMs are measured in parameters (hundreds of millions to trillions) and training data (billions of tokens).
* Common families of LLMs include GPT (OpenAI), PaLM (Google), LLaMA (Meta), and Claude (Anthropic).
* LLMs have various applications, including customer support, content creation, code generation, healthcare, finance, education, and gaming.
