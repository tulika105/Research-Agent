## **Overview**
Agentic frameworks are software toolkits that enable the rapid design, deployment, and governance of autonomous AI agents. These frameworks provide reusable building blocks and orchestration layers, allowing teams to focus on business logic rather than low-level plumbing. The term "agentic" emphasizes the ability of an AI system to set goals, plan, and act autonomously while being governed by human-defined policies.

## **Key Findings**
* Agentic frameworks provide reusable building blocks for perception, memory, planning, action, and communication, enabling rapid development of autonomous AI agents.
* Recent developments include Microsoft's AutoGen + Semantic Kernel, open-source projects such as LangChain-Agentic and OpenAI's Agentic Toolkit, and enterprise adoption by over 60% of Fortune-500 firms.
* Agentic frameworks have a layered design, including input/perception, agent core, memory and state management, planning and decision, action and execution, and orchestration and governance layers.
* Key concepts include the agentic loop, multi-agent coordination, and policy-driven autonomy.
* Real-world applications include customer support, supply-chain and logistics, healthcare, finance, and robotics.

## **Background & Context**
Agentic frameworks have emerged as a crucial component in the development of autonomous AI systems. These frameworks provide a foundation for building AI agents that can reason, plan, and act while remaining under human oversight. The development of agentic frameworks is driven by the need for rapid deployment and governance of autonomous AI agents, which is critical in various industries such as customer support, logistics, and healthcare.

The concept of agentic frameworks is built on the idea of agency, which refers to the ability of an AI system to set goals, plan, and act autonomously. Agentic frameworks provide a set of reusable building blocks and orchestration layers that enable teams to focus on business logic rather than low-level plumbing. This allows for rapid development and deployment of autonomous AI agents, while ensuring that they operate within human-defined policies and guidelines.
